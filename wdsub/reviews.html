10/10 Games

Take everything here with a grain of salt. Although I play alot of video games, I have no qualifications for a profesional review.

Bioshock

  • Gameplay

    The gameplay for Bioshock can be very fun, but can also fell clunky and slow. Playing as a shooter, Bioshock offers a multitude of weapons, such as a revolver, submachine gun, grenade luncher and even a chemial thrower than can act as flamethrower, a freeze gun, or gas gun. The game also offers a variety of super powers caled plasmids like fire, lightning, telekenesis, and shooting bees out of your fingers. Swapping between guns and powers feels slow to do. Thankfully,the developers fixed this in later titles in the franchise.

  • Enviornment/Design

    The city of Rapture is one of the most stunning enviornments I have seen in gaming, holding up even over twenty years later. Traversing the underwater city and looking out at the ocean around you makes the enviornment one of the most beautiful I have ever seen. It can also be one of the most terrifying. The city is run down, causing a lot of the buildings in it to be run down and dark, making it an unnerving setting.

  • Story

    Bioshock's story is very interesting, acting as a crituiqe on Ayn Rand's philosophy of Objectivism, a philosophy stating that the most moral thing to do is to act in one's self interest. The city of Rapture was intended by its creator, Andrew Ryan, to be an objectivist society. The game plays into this sense of objectivism, by forcing you to make hard choices. There are these characters called the little sisters that have these sea slugs in them that create the powerful chemical called ADAM. In order to survive you need this ADAM to upgrade your character and your plasmids. But in order to harvest the most you can get, you have to kill the little sister. So its up to you to decide if yur survival is more important than killing children (To me, it was not). Bioshock is also considered to have one of the best plot twists in all of gaming, and after playing for myself, it is really good.

Cyberpunk 2077

  • Gameplay

    Cyberpunk has a system that allows you to play anyway you want. You want to run in, grab a guy and throw him at another guy? Have at it. You want to hack into a guy's brain and fry it? Have fun. The gameplay combinations for Cyberpunk 2077 are fun, and make you feel like you can't die even on the hardest difficulty. The gameplay is smooth, and even a fight between a few enemies feels like a movie scene.

  • Enviornment/Design

    Night city and the surrounding area is a bustling city of crime, homeless people, and crazed lunatics. Night city is also a very beautiful cityn if you go to the center of it, in more of the richer areas.

  • Story

    Cyberpunk does a very good job of making you feel connected to it's characters. It does even better at ripping them away. Cyberpunk is game that is not afraid to kill of its characters, which is what I love in games. The game also supports a total of five endings, with an extra one added in the Phantom Liberty DLC. A total of zero of these endings are happy, however.

Red Dead Redemption 2

  • Gameplay

    Red Dead redemption plays like any other third person shooter game, with some survival aspects, like needing to eat when your cores (health, stamina, and deadeye) get low. The stand out feature is the deadeye system, allowing you to slow time and mark spots on enemies to automaticaly shoot.

  • Enviornment/Design

    The map in Red Dead Redemption 2 has many places to visit, from snowy mountains, to plains, to a reaching desert. There are some spots in the world that are just beuatiful to look at, rivaling games that release today.

  • Story

    This is the best story in a video game I have ever played. The developement of Arthur Morgan, the main character, Dutch Van Der Linde, and other characters are the best I have seen out of any media. The game is not afraid to kill of charcters, and they do it in such a way that will make you suprised every time. And by the time the game ends, what was once a camp bustling with life turns into a husk of it's old self. The games story acts as a prequel to the first game, meaning you get to see the Dutch's descent into madness as the game goes on.

Grounded

  • Gameplay

    The gameplay of grounded is my favourite part about the game. The game allows you play multiple different ways, from weilding a great club made out of a spider called the broodmother, to making a chandelier out of that same creature. You can weild a bow and arrow or crossbow, which offers a variety of different arrow types, like gas, bombs, and arrows made out of a variety of different candies. You can make armour from the shell of a roly poly, or out of a wasp. The base building system is excelent, allowing you to build huge structures out of grass or mushrooms. The content in this game is of a large amount, and can keep you busy for hours.

  • Enviornment/Design

    The backyard feels like a whole different world than it actually is, and offers variety of different enviornments. From the scorching hot sandbox to the pond in the middle of the yard. The cartoonish aspect of the game is very appealing.

  • Story

    The story is basic but enjoyable and interesting. After being shrunk down to be smaller than a blade of grass, you and three of your friends must find a way to grow back to normal size, all while trying to foil the evil Director Schmectors plans.

Halo: Reach

  • Gameplay

    Plays as the other Halo games, a basic shooter, only with a couple additions tacked onto it. The main new feature is armour abilities. Armour abilities can be found around the levels and can be very different. Some include sprinting, armour lock(temporary invincibility), and invisibility.

  • Enviornment/Design

    The planet Reach is a very nice looking enviornment, with the begining of the game showing it as a planet full of greenery. However, as the game progresses, the levels become less green, and more firery and destroyed due to the Human - Covenant War

  • Story

    Similarly to Red Dead Redemption 2, Halo reach is not afraid to pull its punches when it comes to killing off chracters. Starting in a team with six members, the character you play is eventualy reduced to a lone wolf, as their team is killed off one by one, some quick, like how one is suddenly shot in the head during a cutscene, or dramatic, like how another gives his life to trigger a bomb on a spaceship.
